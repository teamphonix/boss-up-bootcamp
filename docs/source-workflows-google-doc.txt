Workflow 1: Creating an AI Song with Suno
Objective
Create an original song using ChatGPT and Suno AI.
Tools Needed
• ChatGPT (Free or Paid)
• Suno
• Email Account
Step 1: Create Your Suno Account
1. Visit Suno.

2. Create an account.

3. Log in.

Step 2: Generate Song Lyrics


If your not going to write the lyrics yourself you can have the AI of your choice do it for you.
   1. Open ChatGPT.

   2. Describe the song you want.

Example:
Write me a motivational hip hop song about overcoming addiction and finding faith.
      3. Review the lyrics.

      4. Edit anything you want changed.

      5. Copy the final lyrics.

Step 3: Create a Style Prompt
Ask ChatGPT:
Create a Suno style prompt under 500 characters for these lyrics.
Example Output:
Emotional hip hop with cinematic strings, hard drums, inspirational energy, male vocals, powerful chorus, modern production, uplifting atmosphere.
Copy the style prompt.
Step 4: Create the Song in Suno
         1. Open Suno.

         2. Select Custom Mode.

         3. Paste lyrics into Lyrics section.

         4. Paste style prompt into Style section.

         5. Give the song a title.

         6. Click Generate.

Step 5: Review the Results
Suno will generate two versions.
Ask yourself:
• Do I like the beat?
• Do I like the vocals?
• Do I like the arrangement?
• Do I like the emotion?
If yes:
Move to Step 8.
If no:
Move to Step 6.
Step 6: Remix
Use Remix when:
• Lyrics need adjustment
• Different instruments are needed
• Different vocal style is desired
• Better arrangement is needed
Update:
• Lyrics section for lyric changes
• Style section for sound changes
Click Generate again.
Step 7: Generate Additional Versions
If neither version works:
            1. Return to project.

            2. Keep same lyrics and prompt.

            3. Generate again.

Each generation creates new variations.
Continue until satisfied.
Step 8: Export Your Song
Download your final version.
Save:
• Audio file
• Lyrics
• Artwork
• Project notes
Free vs Paid
Free Plan
• Limited generations
• Limited daily credits
Paid Plan ($10+)
• More generations
• Commercial usage rights
• Faster workflow
Bonus Method: Using Your Own Voice
               1. Record yourself singing or rapping.

               2. Upload recording.

               3. Review generated lyrics carefully.

               4. Correct any lyric mistakes.

               5. Add a style prompt.

               6. Generate the finished track.

Final Outcome
You now have:
• Original lyrics
• Original music
• Album-ready audio
• Downloadable song
• Commercial-use content (paid plans)

Workflow 2: Building a Business with AI
Objective
Turn an idea into a legally registered business with a brand, business plan, equipment list, and launch strategy.
Tools Needed
• ChatGPT or preferred AI
• Internet access
• Email account
• State business registration website
• Screenshot capability
________________


Step 1: Choose Your Business Idea
Every business starts with a problem you want to solve.
Examples:
• Recording Studio
• Clothing Brand
• Landscaping Company
• Barber Shop
• Restaurant
• Real Estate Business
• Online Store
Ask AI:
I want to start a recording studio. What does it take to build and operate a successful recording studio business?
AI will provide:
• Startup requirements
• Equipment
• Costs
• Business structure
• Revenue streams
• Potential challenges
________________


Step 2: Build Your Business Plan
Once AI explains the business, ask:
Create a complete business plan for my recording studio.
The AI will generate:
Executive Summary
What the business does.
Target Audience
Who your customers are.
Services
What you’ll sell.
Startup Costs
Expected expenses.
Revenue Sources
How you’ll make money.
Marketing Strategy
How customers will find you.
Growth Strategy
How the business expands.
________________


Step 3: Create Your Brand
Every business needs an identity.
Ask:
Give me 20 business name ideas for a modern recording studio.
Review the options.
Select your favorite.
Then ask:
Create a brand identity for this business.
The AI can help define:
• Colors
• Style
• Tone
• Mission
• Personality
________________


Step 4: Design Your Logo
Once you’ve selected a name:
Ask:
Create logo concepts for my recording studio.
Be specific.
Examples:
• Futuristic
• Luxury
• Urban
• Minimalist
• Retro
• Corporate
• Artistic
Generate multiple versions.
Refine until satisfied.
________________


Step 5: Legally Register Your Business
Ask AI:
What are the legal steps to register a recording studio business in New Jersey?
AI will provide:
• Business structure options
• Registration requirements
• State filing links
• Tax requirements
• EIN requirements
• Licensing requirements
________________


Step 6: Use AI as Your Filing Assistant
This is where most people quit.
Don’t.
As you move through the filing process:
                        1. Take a screenshot.

                        2. Upload it.

                        3. Ask:

What does this page mean?
or
What information do I enter here?
AI can explain each field one step at a time.
This turns complicated paperwork into a guided process.
Real World Example
The H.I.P.H.O.P. Academy nonprofit was built using this exact method.
Instead of guessing:
• Questions were asked
• Screenshots were uploaded
• Each page was explained
• The filing process was completed one step at a time
________________


Step 7: Determine Equipment Needs
Ask:
What equipment do I need to start a recording studio?
AI will provide:
Essential Equipment
Must have.
Recommended Equipment
Helpful upgrades.
Luxury Equipment
Future purchases.
Budget Versions
Affordable alternatives.
________________


Step 8: Create a Startup Budget
Ask:
Create a startup budget for a recording studio with a $5,000 budget.
or
Create a startup budget for a recording studio with a $20,000 budget.
AI can scale recommendations based on available funds.
________________


Step 9: Create a 30 / 60 / 90 Day Action Plan
Ask:
Create a 90-day startup roadmap for my recording studio.
AI will organize:
First 30 Days
Planning and setup.
Days 31-60
Branding and marketing.
Days 61-90
Sales and customer acquisition.
________________


Step 10: Launch
At this point you should have:
✅ Business Idea
✅ Business Plan
✅ Business Name
✅ Logo
✅ Brand Identity
✅ Legal Registration
✅ Equipment List
✅ Startup Budget
✅ 30/60/90 Day Plan
✅ Marketing Strategy
Now it’s time to begin operating.
________________

Workflow 3: Graphic Design with AI
Objective
Learn how to create professional graphics, flyers, logos, social media content, album covers, business branding, and marketing materials using AI.
Tools Needed
• ChatGPT or another AI assistant
• AI image generator
• Photos, screenshots, logos, or reference images (optional)
________________


Step 1: Gather Your Information
Before creating anything, gather the information you want included.
Examples:
Event Flyer
• Event Name
• Date
• Time
• Location
• Contact Information
• Color Scheme
• Theme
Business Promotion
• Business Name
• Services
• Logo
• Brand Colors
• Special Offers
Music Release
• Song Title
• Artist Name
• Theme
• Mood
• Style
The more information you provide, the better the result.
________________


Step 2: Give AI the Information
One of the easiest methods is simply uploading the information.
Examples:
• Screenshot of a text conversation
• Event details
• Existing logo
• Brand image
• Handwritten notes
• Reference designs
Then tell the AI:
“Create a flyer using this information.”
or
“Create a promotional image using this information.”
The AI will organize the information and create a visual concept.
________________


Step 3: Review the First Draft
The first version is rarely the final version.
Review:
• Layout
• Colors
• Typography
• Images
• Accuracy
Ask yourself:
Does this represent what I am trying to communicate?
________________


Step 4: Refine the Design
The most important skill is learning how to give feedback.
Examples:
“Keep everything the same but change the colors.”
“Keep the layout but make it more modern.”
“Remove the image on the right side.”
“Use gold accents instead of silver.”
“Make it look more luxurious.”
“Make it look futuristic.”
“Make it look more professional.”
Be specific.
The AI responds best when given clear direction.
________________


Step 5: Create Branding Assets
Once you understand the process, you can create:
Logos
Ask:
“Create a logo for a modern recording studio.”
Business Graphics
Ask:
“Create a promotional image representing this company.”
Album Covers
Ask:
“Create an album cover representing this song.”
Book Covers
Ask:
“Create a book cover using this title and theme.”
Social Media Posts
Ask:
“Create an Instagram promotional graphic for this business.”
________________


Step 6: Improve Existing Designs
You do not need to start from scratch.
Upload:
• Existing logos
• Old flyers
• Business cards
• Photos
Then ask:
“Modernize this logo.”
“Enhance this flyer.”
“Turn this sketch into a professional design.”
“Make this image look like a luxury brand advertisement.”
AI can improve what already exists.
________________


Step 7: Use References
The fastest way to get what you want is by showing examples.
Upload:
• Color palettes
• Existing logos
• Photos
• Flyers you like
• Brand assets
Then tell AI:
“I want something similar to this style.”
References dramatically improve results.
________________


Step 8: Final Export
Once approved:
• Download the design
• Save source assets
• Create alternate versions
• Create versions for social media
• Create versions for print
Always save multiple versions.
________________


Key Lesson
You do not need to know how to draw.
You do not need to know Photoshop.
You do not need to know Illustrator.
Your job is to communicate your vision clearly.
The AI becomes the designer.
You become the creative director.
The better you describe what you want, the better the final result will be.
Workflow 4: Building a Website with AI
Objective
Take everything you’ve already created—your business plan, brand, logo, graphics, products, music, and services—and bring them together into one digital experience: your website.
________________


What A Website Really Is
A website is where everything you’ve built comes together.
By this point you should already have:
• A business idea
• A business name
• A logo
• Brand colors
• Graphics
• Promotional images
• Products or services
• A mission statement
• Content about your business
Now we’re going to put it all in one place.
________________


Step 1: Gather Your Assets
Before building the website, gather everything you’ve already created.
Examples:
• Logo
• Business name
• Mission statement
• About Us information
• Product descriptions
• Service descriptions
• Promotional graphics
• Music
• Videos
• Contact information
If you’re missing something, ask AI to create it.
Example:
“Create an About Us section for my recording studio.”
or
“Create a mission statement for my nonprofit.”
________________


Step 2: Create Website Assets
Websites need special graphics.
Ask your AI designer to create:
• PNG versions of logos
• Website icons
• Header graphics
• Background images
• Service cards
• Buttons
• Banners
Example:
“Remove the background from this logo and create a PNG version.”
or
“Turn this logo into a simple website icon.”
________________


Step 3: Create The Website Vision
Now tell your AI website builder exactly what you’re trying to build.
Example:
“I want a website for my recording studio.”
or
“I want a website for my nonprofit.”
Then explain:
• What the business does
• Who it’s for
• What pages you want
• What people should be able to do
Examples:
• Listen to music
• Book appointments
• Buy products
• Submit forms
• Donate
• Contact you
The more specific you are, the better the result.
________________


Step 4: Create A Wireframe
Before building the website, ask:
“Show me a wireframe for this website.”
Think of a wireframe like a blueprint.
It shows:
• Homepage layout
• Navigation menu
• Buttons
• Sections
• Forms
• Footer
Review it.
Move things around.
Make changes.
Once you’re happy with it, move on.
________________


Step 5: Build The Website
Now tell the AI:
“Build the website.”
The AI will begin creating:
• Home page
• About page
• Contact page
• Service pages
• Product pages
• Forms
• Menus
• Buttons
• Layouts
This is where your assets begin getting placed throughout the website.
________________


Step 6: Direct The Design
Do not be afraid to tell the AI exactly what you want.
Examples:
“Use this image as the homepage background.”
“Move this section higher.”
“Make the buttons gold.”
“Add a music player.”
“Create a booking form.”
“Make the page feel more modern.”
Think of the AI like a web designer sitting next to you.
Talk to it.
Direct it.
Correct it.
Refine it.
________________


Step 7: Add Functionality
Now decide what visitors should be able to do.
Examples:
Contact Forms
People can send messages.
Booking Forms
People can schedule appointments.
Music Players
Visitors can listen to your music.
Product Sales
Customers can purchase items.
Donations
Supporters can donate.
Downloads
People can download files.
Tell the AI what you want.
It will explain what tools are required.
________________


Step 8: Create A GitHub Account
At this point your project is becoming real.
Now is when I create my GitHub account.
I personally do not start with GitHub.
I usually build first.
Then I create GitHub.
Then I connect it to the AI builder.
Examples:
• Codex
• Hermes
• Claude
• Cursor
• Replit
• Vercel
I simply tell the AI:
“Create a repository for this project.”
The AI handles most of the heavy lifting.
________________


Step 9: Use GitHub As Your Project Backup
I don’t use GitHub because I’m a programmer.
I use GitHub because I don’t want to lose my work.
Once connected, the AI can store:
• Website files
• Graphics
• Assets
• Updates
• Changes
• Future improvements
Now if you switch computers, switch AI platforms, or return six months later, your project still exists.
________________


Step 10: Connect Hosting
Once the website looks good, publish it.
Common options:
• Vercel
• Netlify
• GitHub Pages
The AI will usually walk you through this process.
If something is confusing:
Take a screenshot.
Upload it.
Ask:
“What do I do here?”
________________


Step 11: Connect Your Domain
Purchase a domain.
Examples:
• HipHopAcademyNJ.org
• CatchyCorp.com
• TwentySevenSounds.com
Connect the domain to your hosting provider.
Now your website has a professional address.
________________


Step 12: Test Everything
Before launching, test:
• Buttons
• Forms
• Mobile version
• Downloads
• Music players
• Videos
• Payment systems
• Navigation menus
If something doesn’t work:
Take a screenshot.
Show the AI.
Let it help fix it.
________________


Key Lesson
You do not need to know how to code.
You do not need to know how to design websites.
You do not need to know GitHub.
You need to know how to explain your vision.
The AI helps build.
You direct.
The better you communicate what you want, the better your website becomes.
Your business plan created the business.
Your graphics created the brand.
Your website brings everything together into one place where people can experience what you’ve built.


Workflow 5: Turning Images Into Videos with AI
Objective
Learn how to take images, flyers, logos, graphics, album covers, business advertisements, and personal photos and transform them into animated videos for marketing, social media, websites, and music videos.
________________


Why This Matters
Most people stop after creating an image.
Smart creators take that same image and turn it into video content.
One image can become:
• A commercial
• A social media ad
• A music video scene
• A website background
• A promotional video
• A product showcase
• An event advertisement
Instead of creating more content, you create more value from the content you already have.
________________


Step 1: Create Your Image
Start with any image.
Examples:
• Flyer
• Album cover
• Logo
• Business card
• Advertisement
• AI artwork
• Family photo
• Self portrait
• Product image
The better the image, the better the animation.
________________


Step 2: Upload The Image To An AI Video Generator
I personally like Grok because it is fast and simple.
Upload your image.
Then tell it what you want to happen.
Examples:
"Bring this image to life."
"Create movement in this scene."
"Animate the lights and camera movement."
"Make the character walk forward."
"Make the balloons float into the sky."
"Make this look like a movie scene."
The AI will create a short animated clip from your image.
________________


Step 3: Review The Animation
The AI usually creates a short scene.
Review:
• Motion
• Camera movement
• Lighting
• Effects
• Realism
If it isn't what you wanted:
Adjust the prompt.
Generate again.
Repeat until satisfied.
________________


Step 4: Extend The Scene
One of my favorite techniques is extending scenes.
After the video is generated:
Take a screenshot from the final moment of the clip.
Use that screenshot as the starting point for the next scene.
Then tell the AI:
"Continue this scene."
or
"Continue from where this left off."
Now the animation continues naturally.
________________


Step 5: Create Longer Sequences
Repeat the process.
Scene 1
↓
Screenshot Last Frame
↓
Scene 2
↓
Screenshot Last Frame
↓
Scene 3
↓
Screenshot Last Frame
↓
Scene 4
Before long, a single image has become a complete visual sequence.
________________


Step 6: Build A Story
Now start thinking like a director.
Ask:
What happens next?
Examples:
Business Advertisement
Store exterior
↓
Customer enters
↓
Product showcase
↓
Call to action
Music Video
Character walking
↓
Rain scene
↓
Emotional close-up
↓
Final performance scene
Event Promotion
Venue exterior
↓
Decorations
↓
Guests arriving
↓
Event information
________________


Step 7: Assemble Everything In A Video Editor
I personally use CapCut.
Import:
• Video clips
• Images
• Music
• Voiceovers
• Sound effects
Arrange them into a timeline.
Add transitions.
Adjust timing.
Create a finished presentation.
________________


Step 8: Add Music
You can use music you already created.
For example:
Create a song in Suno.
Download the audio.
Import it into CapCut.
Place it underneath your video scenes.
Now your visuals and music work together.
________________


Step 9: Create Different Types Of Content
The same workflow can be used for:
Business Advertisements
Promote products and services.
Music Videos
Create visuals for songs.
Social Media Posts
Create short attention-grabbing clips.
Event Promotions
Animate flyers and invitations.
Website Content
Create motion backgrounds and hero sections.
Personal Projects
Bring photos and artwork to life.
________________


Real World Example
A flyer does not have to stay a flyer.
A business card does not have to stay a business card.
A logo does not have to stay a logo.
Take the image.
Animate it.
Extend the scene.
Add music.
Edit it together.
Now you have a commercial.
________________


Key Lesson
Images are no longer the final product.
Images are the starting point.
A single graphic can become:
• A video
• An advertisement
• A music video
• A social media campaign
• A website animation
• A complete marketing asset
The goal is not just creating content.
The goal is turning one piece of content into many pieces of content.
Bonus Lesson: Using ChatGPT to Improve Video Prompts
One mistake people make is trying to write the perfect video prompt themselves.
You don’t have to.
If you’re struggling to get the results you want from a video generator like Grok, go back to ChatGPT and explain what you’re trying to create.
Think of ChatGPT as your prompt engineer.
________________


My Workflow
Instead of writing a complicated video prompt myself, I simply explain my idea.
Example:
“I want a man falling into the ocean. The camera starts close to his face, slowly zooms out as he falls, follows him into the water, and then transitions to an underwater view.”
That’s it.
I don’t worry about technical terms.
I just explain the scene.
________________


Let ChatGPT Fill In The Missing Details
ChatGPT understands visual storytelling.
It can automatically add details like:
• Camera angles
• Camera movement
• Lighting
• Cinematic effects
• Environmental details
• Atmosphere
• Realism
• Depth of field
• Lens effects
• Motion descriptions
• Scene transitions
These are things many beginners don’t even know to ask for.
________________


Example
Instead of:
“Make a guy fall into the ocean.”
You can ask ChatGPT:
“Turn this into a detailed AI video prompt.”
The result might become something like:
“A cinematic close-up of a man falling toward a vast ocean at sunset. The camera begins tightly focused on his face, capturing emotion and wind movement, then slowly pulls back as he descends. Golden sunlight reflects across the water below. As he impacts the ocean surface, a dramatic splash fills the frame before the perspective transitions underwater, showing bubbles rising and light rays piercing through the water. Realistic physics, cinematic camera movement, high detail, emotional atmosphere, film-quality visuals.”
Now the video generator has much more information to work with.
________________


Keep Your Instructions Simple
You do not need to sound like a filmmaker.
You do not need to know technical camera terms.
Just explain:
• What is happening
• Who is involved
• What the environment looks like
• What emotion you want
• What the camera should do
Then let ChatGPT translate your idea into a professional prompt.
________________


Key Lesson
Use one AI to help another AI.
ChatGPT can help create:
• Video prompts
• Image prompts
• Music prompts
• Website prompts
• Coding prompts
• Business prompts
• Marketing prompts
You do not need to master every tool.
You need to learn how to make the tools work together.
That is where the real power comes from.
